Experience

***WIP***

I received my B.A. in Economics with a minor in Philosophy from the Morrissey College of Arts/Sciences at Boston College.

My favorite courses were a Logic course in Philosophy, Econometric Methods, and Business Organizational Behavior.

Logic covered boolean logic and conditional interactions in depth. I was deeply fascinated with the prevalence of logical operations in nearly every facet of life. Web and Software development is then just a concrete application of these abstract principles.

Econometric Methods was the most challenging course, demanding a deep understanding of regression models and their applications in real life. My final project involved manipulating South African census data via Stata to determine the health of a new-born child given over 30 variables.

Business Organizational Behavior focused on many aspects of how good companies are structured and operate , but the most valuable experience I got from this class was my reading on what makes a good leader, a good team, and a good team member.


About


I've always had a deep passion for technology, with dreams of working on a compelling product. Web design more deeply taps into my desire to create things, and provides endless opportunities for ingenuity.

Coding allows me to think outside the box exersize my creativity whilst applying my mathematically oriented mind in endlessly unique ways.

I love to build computers with custom water-cooling loops, and have recently been planning on building a DIY drone from scratch.

Originally my interest lied in the construction and design aspects of the project, but web development has opened my eyes to the world of actually programming some of the controller functionalities!
